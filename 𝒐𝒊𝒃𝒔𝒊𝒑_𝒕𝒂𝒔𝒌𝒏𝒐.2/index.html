Mahendra Singh Dhoni

   

Former Indian Captain

Mahendra Singh Dhoni born 7 July 1981 Ranchi ,Jharkhand

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, affectionately known as "MS Dhoni," is a cricketing legend and one of the most revered figures in the sport. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, India, Dhoni's journey from a small-town boy to the captain of the Indian cricket team is an inspiring tale of determination and resilience. Dhoni is widely recognized for his exceptional leadership skills and composed demeanor on the field. As a wicketkeeper-batsman, he revolutionized the role of a finisher in limited-overs cricket, earning the moniker "Captain Cool" for his calm and calculated approach to high-pressure situations. His ability to stay unruffled under intense pressure and steer his team to victory in nail-biting situations earned him the respect and admiration of fans worldwide.

Some facts about MSD:

  • Mahendra Singh Dhoni was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India.
  • Dhoni made his debut for the Indian cricket team in December 2004 during an ODI match against Bangladesh in Chittagong.
  • He is one of the most successful Indian cricket captains, leading the team to victories in three major ICC tournaments: the ICC World T20 in 2007, the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.
  • Dhoni is known for his exceptional wicketkeeping skills and has set numerous records behind the stumps. He holds the record for most dismissals by an Indian wicketkeeper in all formats of the game.
  • Dhoni holds the record for the most not-outs in One Day Internationals (ODIs) and is one of the most successful ODI captains in history.
  • Under his captaincy, India achieved the number one ranking in Test cricket for the first time in December 2009.
  • Apart from cricket, Dhoni has a passion for motorcycles and holds a collection of high-end bikes.
  • In 2020, Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket, marking the end of an illustrious and influential career that left an indelible mark on Indian cricket.
